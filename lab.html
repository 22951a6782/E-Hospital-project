Available Lab Tests and Their Purposes

Complete Blood Count (CBC)
Evaluates overall health and detects a wide range of disorders.
Basic Metabolic Panel (BMP)
Assesses kidney function, electrolyte levels, and blood sugar levels.
Lipid Panel
Measures cholesterol levels to assess the risk of heart disease.
Thyroid Stimulating Hormone (TSH)
Evaluates thyroid function.
Urinalysis
Checks for urinary tract infections, kidney diseases, and diabetes.
Liver Function Tests (LFTs)
Measures liver enzymes and proteins to assess liver function.
Blood Glucose (Fasting and Random)
Measures blood sugar levels to diagnose diabetes.
Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c)
Monitors long-term blood sugar levels in diabetes management.
Prothrombin Time (PT)
Measures how long it takes for blood to clot to assess bleeding risks.
Activated Partial Thromboplastin Time (aPTT)
Evaluates blood clotting function.
Electrolyte Panel (Sodium, Potassium, Chloride, Bicarbonate)
Assesses electrolyte balance.
C-reactive Protein (CRP)
Indicates inflammation in the body.
Blood Culture
Detects infections in the bloodstream.
Serum Iron and Total Iron-Binding Capacity (TIBC)
Evaluates iron levels and iron-binding capacity.
Thyroid Function Tests (T3, T4, Free T4, TSH)
Assesses thyroid hormone levels.
